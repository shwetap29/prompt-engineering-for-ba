# 🚀 Your First 5 Prompts

**Level:** Beginner  
**Time:** Try one today, one per day this week

---

These are the 5 most immediately useful prompts for any BA or DA.  
Each one is ready to copy, paste, and fill in right now.

---

## Prompt 1 — Turn Meeting Notes into Action Items

**When:** After any stakeholder call or workshop  
**Time saved:** 20–30 minutes

```
You are a Business Analyst summarising a stakeholder meeting.

From the notes below, extract and structure:
1. Key decisions made (bullet list)
2. Action items — as a table with columns: Action | Owner | Due Date
3. Open questions that still need answers

Keep the language plain and professional.

Meeting notes:
[PASTE YOUR NOTES HERE]
```

---

## Prompt 2 — Explain Data to a Non-Technical Stakeholder

**When:** You have numbers that need to become a story  
**Time saved:** 30–40 minutes

```
You are a Data Analyst explaining results to a senior business stakeholder 
who does not have a data background.

Write 3–4 sentences that:
- Lead with the single most important finding
- Use plain language (no technical terms)
- Include the actual numbers from the data
- End with one clear recommendation

Data:
[PASTE YOUR TABLE OR KPI RESULTS HERE]

What this data is tracking:
[ONE SENTENCE OF CONTEXT]
```

---

## Prompt 3 — Write a Professional Email Fast

**When:** You need to send a status update, follow-up, or request  
**Time saved:** 15–20 minutes

```
You are a Business Analyst writing a professional email.

Write a concise email (under 150 words) with:
- A clear subject line
- One opening sentence stating the purpose
- 2–3 bullet points with the key information
- A single, specific call to action at the end

Context:
[DESCRIBE WHAT THE EMAIL IS ABOUT IN 2–3 SENTENCES]

Recipient: [WHO IS RECEIVING IT — e.g., "a non-technical project sponsor"]
Tone: professional but not stiff — clear and direct
```

---

## Prompt 4 — Review Something You've Written

**When:** You've drafted a document and want a quick quality check  
**Time saved:** 20–30 minutes

```
You are a senior Business Analyst reviewing a colleague's work.

Review the document below and give feedback on:
1. Clarity — is anything confusing or ambiguous?
2. Completeness — what is missing?
3. Tone — is it appropriate for the intended audience?
4. Suggested improvements — rewrite any weak sections

Document:
[PASTE YOUR DRAFT HERE]

Intended audience: [WHO WILL READ THIS]
```

---

## Prompt 5 — Simplify Something Complicated

**When:** You need to explain a technical concept, process, or result  
in plain language  
**Time saved:** 20–25 minutes

```
You are a Business Analyst who is excellent at explaining complex topics 
in simple terms.

Explain the following in plain language that a non-technical business 
stakeholder with no data background can understand.

- Use an analogy if it helps
- Avoid jargon — if a technical term is unavoidable, define it in brackets
- Keep it to 3–5 sentences maximum

Topic to explain:
[PASTE YOUR COMPLEX CONCEPT, PROCESS, OR RESULT HERE]
```

---

## Tips for Your First Week

- **Don't overthink it.** Paste a messy draft, imperfect notes, or a rough idea — AI works with what you give it.
- **Read the output critically.** Always check facts, names, and numbers — AI can make confident mistakes.
- **Iterate.** If the first response isn't right, type *"make it more concise"* or *"add a section on risks"* — don't start over.
- **Save your best prompts.** When a prompt works really well, save it in a doc for next time.
