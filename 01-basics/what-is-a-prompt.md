# 💬 What Is a Prompt?

**Level:** Beginner  
**Time to read:** 5 minutes

---

## The Simple Answer

A prompt is just the instruction you give to an AI tool like Claude or ChatGPT.

Every time you type something into an AI chat window, that's a prompt. The difference between a vague answer and a great one is almost always **how well the prompt was written** — not the AI itself.

---

## A Bad Prompt vs. A Good Prompt

### ❌ Vague Prompt
```
Write a requirements document.
```
**What AI does:** Produces something generic with no context, 
wrong format, wrong audience, and you rewrite most of it.

---

### ✅ Specific Prompt
```
You are a senior Business Analyst. Write a Business Requirements Document 
section for a new weekly sales report. The audience is both the development 
team and a non-technical Finance Director. Include: Business Objective, 
Scope (In/Out), Functional Requirements (numbered), and Assumptions.
```
**What AI does:** Produces a structured, audience-appropriate BRD 
section you can edit and use in under 10 minutes.

---

## Why This Matters for BAs and DAs

As a BA or DA, you already know how to:
- Ask the right questions
- Structure information clearly
- Write for different audiences

Prompting is exactly the same skill — applied to AI.  
You are not "talking to a computer." You are briefing a very fast assistant who needs clear instructions to do great work.

---

## The 3 Things Every Prompt Needs

| Element | What It Does | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Role** | Tells AI who to be | *"You are a senior BA..."* |
| **Task** | Tells AI what to do | *"Write a BRD section..."* |
| **Context** | Gives AI what it needs | *"Based on these meeting notes: [paste notes]"* |

Optional but powerful:
| Element | What It Does | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Format** | Tells AI how to structure output | *"Use a numbered list and headers"* |
| **Audience** | Tells AI who will read this | *"Write for a non-technical Finance Director"* |
| **Tone** | Tells AI the style | *"Professional but concise — no jargon"* |

---

## Your First Exercise

Open ChatGPT, Claude, or Copilot. Try both versions below and compare the outputs:

**Version A (vague):**
```
Summarise this meeting.
[paste any meeting notes]
```

**Version B (specific):**
```
You are a Business Analyst summarising a stakeholder meeting.
From the notes below, extract:
- 3 key decisions made
- All action items (with owner and due date if mentioned)
- Any open questions that need follow-up

Meeting notes:
[paste your meeting notes]
```

Notice the difference? That's the power of a well-written prompt.
